About the practice

Meet Dr. Emily Carter

A warm, collaborative approach grounded in research and shaped by real-life experience.

Dr. Emily Carter, PhD

Licensed Clinical Psychologist · License #PSY12345

Over 15 years of clinical experience with adults and emerging adults.

Integrates CBT, mindfulness-based approaches, and compassion-focused therapy.

Specializes in anxiety, burnout, life transitions, and self-esteem struggles.

I believe meaningful change happens in a space where you feel seen, safe, and free from judgment. Together we’ll gently slow down, make sense of what you’re going through, and develop practical tools you can bring into your everyday life.

With a doctorate in Clinical Psychology from Stanford University, I’ve worked with hundreds of clients navigating anxiety, burnout, relationship stress, and major life transitions. My style is collaborative and gentle, yet focused and structured when that’s helpful.

Whether you’re feeling “not yourself,” stuck in unhelpful patterns, or simply curious about knowing yourself more deeply, therapy can be a powerful space to reconnect with who you are and how you want to live.

Ways we can work together

Thoughtfully designed support so we can tailor our work to your life, schedule, and goals.

Individual therapy

One-on-one sessions focused on building insight, coping tools, and a more compassionate relationship with yourself.

From $150 · 50 minutes

Common focuses include:

  • Managing anxiety, worry, and a loud inner critic.
  • Burnout, perfectionism, and feeling overwhelmed by expectations.

Couples therapy

A structured, supportive space to strengthen communication, repair trust, and deepen connection.

From $180 · 50 minutes

Often helpful for:

  • Recurring arguments or feeling “stuck” in the same patterns.
  • Healing after painful chapters or breaches of trust.
  • Building shared language around needs, boundaries, and repair.

Online therapy

Secure video sessions that bring the same depth of work into the comfort of your own space.

From $140 · 50 minutes

Online sessions include:

  • Secure, HIPAA-compliant platform and simple tech setup.

  • Access from anywhere within the state of California.

Practical details

Frequently asked questions

Helpful information as you decide whether this is the right fit for you.

Individual sessions are $150 for 50 minutes; couples sessions are $180. A limited number of reduced-fee slots are available based on financial need, and we can talk about this openly.
I’m an out-of-network provider for all plans. Many PPOs offer partial reimbursement for out-of-network therapy. I can provide a monthly superbill you can submit to your insurance for possible reimbursement.
It depends on your goals. Some people come for 8–12 sessions around a specific concern, while others prefer longer-term work. We’ll decide together and revisit regularly to make sure therapy is still serving you.
We’ll talk about what’s bringing you in, your history, and what you’d like to get out of therapy. You’re always in charge of what you share and at what pace. You can also ask questions about how I work.
Research suggests that for many concerns, online therapy can be just as effective as in-person sessions. The quality of the therapeutic relationship and your engagement are what matter most.
