# 3DVR Segment Angles

Date: 2026-03-31

Use these as the default page and pitch angles for the first three paid wedges.

## Segment 1: Professional Services
### Who
- consultants
- creative studios
- marketers
- coaches
- small agencies

### Core Pain
- leads come in from too many places
- follow-up is inconsistent
- proposals and next steps stall
- the founder is selling and delivering at the same time

### Outcome Promise
Get a simple system for leads, follow-up, and weekly operating rhythm so fewer opportunities go cold.

### Best Hook
Stop losing warm leads between the first message and the signed job.

### Headline Angles
- Keep warm leads moving without building a giant sales stack.
- A simpler operating system for small service businesses that need more follow-up, not more software.
- Get organized, follow up faster, and turn more conversations into paid work.

### Proof Angle
- show inbox-to-CRM-to-follow-up flow
- show one founder running the week from one place
- emphasize less switching and fewer dropped conversations

### CTA
- `See the Builder plan`
- `Talk through your sales workflow`

## Segment 2: Local Service Businesses
### Who
- contractors
- installers
- cleaners
- landscapers
- repair and field-service operators

### Core Pain
- missed calls and quote requests
- intake is scattered across phone, text, and email
- follow-up happens late or not at all
- the owner is always in the field

### Outcome Promise
Capture leads faster, keep estimates moving, and run the week from one simple desk.

### Best Hook
Miss fewer quote requests and follow up before the job goes to someone else.

### Headline Angles
- Stop letting calls, texts, and estimates slip through the cracks.
- A lightweight follow-up and operations desk for small local service teams.
- Keep jobs moving without living inside five different apps.

### Proof Angle
- show call/text lead capture
- show quote follow-up cadence
- show weekly schedule and payment flow

### CTA
- `Open the Builder workflow`
- `Talk through your intake and follow-up`

## Segment 3: Small Support Teams And Community Organizations
### Who
- training groups
- care and support teams
- local nonprofits
- membership communities
- education or service programs

### Core Pain
- people, notes, schedules, and follow-up live in too many places
- the team needs accountability and rhythm
- members or clients slip through weak coordination

### Outcome Promise
Give the team one calm system for people, follow-up, scheduling, and shared weekly action.

### Best Hook
Run support and outreach with more consistency and less admin chaos.

### Headline Angles
- One simple operating system for small support teams that need better follow-through.
- Keep people, follow-up, and weekly action in one shared rhythm.
- Help your team stay organized without forcing everyone into a complex platform.

### Proof Angle
- show shared queue and follow-up cadence
- show small-team coordination and accountability
- emphasize clarity and continuity, not "productivity"

### CTA
- `See the Embedded plan`
- `Talk through your team workflow`

## Universal Messaging Rules
- Lead with the pain the buyer already feels.
- Promise a clearer operating rhythm, not "digital transformation."
- Say what gets simpler:
  - lead capture
  - follow-up
  - scheduling
  - payments
  - weekly coordination
- Keep the first screen short.
- Make the paid lane easy to find.

## What To Avoid
- leading with `Life`, `Cell`, or other internal names on market-facing pages
- broad "all-in-one" claims with no concrete pain
- long philosophy before the buyer sees the use case
- promising enterprise-level automation
