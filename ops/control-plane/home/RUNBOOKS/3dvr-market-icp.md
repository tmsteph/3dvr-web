# 3DVR Market ICP

Date: 2026-03-31

## Main Decision
3DVR should sell primarily to small operators who already feel pain around leads, follow-up, scheduling, and keeping the business moving.

The paid center of the business is not "everyone who feels stuck."
That audience matters, but mainly as trust-building top of funnel.

## Primary Paid ICP
### Core Buyer
- owner-led service business
- `1-10` employees or contractors
- founder or operator still close to sales and delivery
- already using some mix of text, notes, calendar, spreadsheet, Stripe, or DMs to run the business

### What They Are Trying To Fix
- not enough consistent leads
- weak follow-up after first contact
- scattered intake and scheduling
- too many disconnected tools
- no clear weekly operating rhythm

### What They Will Pay For
- a simpler system that helps them get more replies, close more work, and stay organized
- direct help getting the system set up and used
- a light operating cadence they can actually keep up with

## Acquisition Audience
### Free Audience
- people who feel lost, stuck, or scattered
- people trying to get clear on what to build next
- people who may later want to launch an offer, service, or small business

### Business Role
- trust builder
- lead qualifier
- future Founder, Builder, or service-work pipeline

Do not treat this free audience as the main paid market.

## Best Near-Term Segments
1. Professional services
   - consultants
   - marketers
   - designers
   - creative studios
   - coaches
   - small agencies
2. Local service businesses
   - contractors
   - installers
   - cleaners
   - landscapers
   - repair services
3. Small support teams and community organizations
   - education and training groups
   - care and support teams
   - local nonprofits
   - membership communities

## Good-Fit Signals
- founder says they need more customers or more consistent follow-up
- business already has some real activity, but the process is messy
- owner is still doing too much by hand
- the team can commit to a simple weekly rhythm
- they want direct help, not just another app login

## Bad-Fit Signals
- enterprise buying process
- heavy inventory, warehouse, or manufacturing requirements
- deep custom ERP expectations
- buyer only wants the cheapest CRM possible
- no real need to follow up, schedule, or coordinate work

## Offer Mapping
### Free
- job: help confused or scattered people get organized
- next step: sort for business intent

### Founder `$20`
- job: help an individual shape an offer, page, or first launch
- use when the buyer is early and still forming the thing

### Builder `$50`
- job: help a solo operator or very small team run sales and operating rhythm with less chaos
- main recurring lane

### Embedded `$200`
- job: support a real team that needs recurring coordination and monthly execution help
- higher-touch recurring lane

### One-Time Deposit
- job: close near-term revenue when the buyer wants a sprint, setup, migration, or launch push
- use as a feeder into Builder or Embedded when possible

## Qualification Questions
Ask these before pushing a paid plan:
1. How are leads coming in now?
2. Where does follow-up usually break?
3. What tools are you juggling today?
4. What kind of work are you trying to sell more of?
5. Are you mostly trying to get clear, get organized, or grow revenue?
6. Are you running this solo or with a team?
7. What would make monthly support worth paying for?

## Market Statement
3DVR helps small service businesses and support teams get organized, follow up consistently, and grow revenue without stitching together a messy tool stack.

## Working Rule
Lead the market with pain and outcomes:
- get more replies
- keep follow-up moving
- stay organized
- run the business with less chaos

Do not lead with internal product names.
