# 3DVR Market Interviews And Outreach

Date: 2026-03-31

This is the default research and sales loop for the current 3DVR wedge.

## Goal
Learn fast which segment closes, what pain shows up most often, and which offer earns the clearest yes.

## Weekly Research Target
- `5` conversations in professional services
- `5` conversations in local services
- `5` conversations in support teams or community organizations

Do not wait for perfect sample size. Start hearing objections now.

## Interview Script
Use this in calls, DMs, or structured email threads. Keep it conversational.

1. What kind of work do you do, and how big is the team?
2. How are new leads, clients, or members coming in today?
3. Where does follow-up usually break down?
4. What tools are you using to keep track of people and next steps?
5. What part of running the week feels most chaotic right now?
6. Are you trying to get more customers, stay organized, or support people better?
7. What have you already tried that did not really stick?
8. If one thing got simpler this month, what would matter most?
9. Would you pay monthly for help fixing that, or would you rather pay for a one-time setup?
10. If I showed you a lighter system for this, would you want to see it?

## Discovery Notes To Capture
- segment
- team size
- lead source
- main pain
- current tools
- urgency
- budget shape
- whether they want software, help, or both

## First Outreach Script
Use a short opener. Do not pitch the whole product in message one.

### Professional Services
Subject or opener:
`Quick question: how are you keeping warm leads moving right now?`

Body:
`I am talking with small service operators who are juggling follow-up across email, text, notes, and calendar. I am building a simpler system for that. If this is a real pain for you, I would like to compare notes for 15 minutes.`

### Local Services
Subject or opener:
`Are quote requests and follow-up getting scattered for you?`

Body:
`I am working on a lightweight sales-and-ops desk for small local service businesses that need faster follow-up without adding a huge software stack. If that is a problem you feel, I would like to hear how you are handling it now.`

### Support Teams
Subject or opener:
`How is your team handling follow-up and weekly coordination right now?`

Body:
`I am talking with small support teams that need a simpler way to keep people, notes, and follow-up moving week to week. If that sounds familiar, I would like to ask a few questions and see where the workflow breaks.`

## Follow-Up Script
Use this after no reply for `3-5` days.

`Following up in case this is a real issue on your side. I am not trying to sell you a huge platform. I am trying to understand where small teams lose momentum on leads, follow-up, and weekly coordination. If it is easier, I can send the questions by email.`

## Offer Transition Script
Use only after pain is clear.

`Based on what you described, the first thing I would tighten is your lead and follow-up rhythm. I can either help you set that up as a one-time sprint, or we can run it as ongoing support through the Builder lane. Which shape is closer to what you need right now?`

## Weekly Review
At the end of each week, count:
- outreach attempts
- replies
- meetings
- paid closes
- one-time deposit opportunities

Then answer:
1. Which segment replied most?
2. Which pain showed up most?
3. Which offer got the clearest traction?
4. Which message opener earned the best response?
5. What do we drop next week?
